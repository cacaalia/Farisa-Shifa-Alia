Tips&Trick

Taking care of your skin requires more than just the right products; habits and daily routines also play an important role. Here are some simple tips and tricks to help you keep your skin healthy, glowing, and well-maintained throughout the day.

Cleanse Your Face Twice a Day

Cleansing your face in the morning and before bed helps remove dirt, oil, and makeup residue that can clog pores.

Use Sunscreen Every Day

Protect your skin from UV rays with sunscreen that has at least SPF 30, even on cloudy days.

Exfoliate Regularly

Exfoliate 1-2 times a week to remove dead skin cells and make your skin look brighter.

Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle

Consume nutritious foods, drink plenty of water, and get enough sleep to support skin health from the inside.

Avoid Touching Your Face Too Often

Hands often carry germs and oils that can cause acne. Keep this habit in check for cleaner skin.

Use Products Suitable for Your Skin Type

Identify your skin type (dry, oily, combination, or sensitive) and choose skincare products that match it.

Regularly Use Face Masks

Face masks provide extra nourishment for your skin. Use masks according to your skin's needs, such as hydration or oil control.

Don't Forget to Moisturize Your Skin

Even oily skin needs moisturizer! Use a lightweight, fast-absorbing moisturizer.

Type Of Skin

Understanding skin types is essential for healthy skin, as each type has specific needs. The main types are normal, oily, dry, combination, and sensitive, each with unique characteristics like oil production and sensitivity. Identifying your skin type helps in choosing the right skincare products and creating an effective routine.



Normal Skin

Balanced skin that is neither too dry nor too oily. It is characterized by a smooth texture, small pores, and is not prone to irritation.

Oily Skin

Skin that produces excess oil, especially in the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin). It is characterized by an oily shine, large pores, and is more prone to acne.

Dry Skin

Skin that lacks moisture, often feeling tight, flaky, or rough. It appears dull, is easily irritated, and frequently experiences peeling.

Combination Skin

A mix of different skin types on various areas of the face, typically with an oily T-zone and drier cheeks. Proper care is needed to balance these areas.

Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin reacts to external factors, causing redness, itching, or burning sensations due to triggers like weather, products, or allergies.
